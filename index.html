RESEARCH

Our team began our research by contacting the organization to find out their major concerns and pain points. We discovered that there was a decrease in adoption rates which leads to sheltered pet euthanization.

Furthermore, it was noted that the website had a lack of updated pet information and pictures of the available animals.

Throughout the research, it became a goal to:

  • Curate an easier navigation system by developing a delightful user interfact to promote more pet adoptions
  • Present updated and inviting pet pictures, along with pet information
  • Offer more personalized search options to improve adoption rates
  • Comeptitive Analysis
  • Persona
  • Interviews

Competitive Analysis

After learning more about the organization and their needs, it was time to compare what San Anotnio Humane Society’s shelter and pet finder function has to offer in comparison to other competitors.

It was found that competitors has little search filters, and poor adoption navigations. In addition, some competitors had no pet finder tools.

Persona

To focus on user-centric design, we developed a user persona to have a clear understanding of who the SA Humane Society’s users are.

With Gerald, our user persona, our team was able to proceed with our interviews and goals for our redesign project.

User Interviews

In order to learn about the real experiences people have had while searching for their fur-ever pet, we conducted user interviews by asking open-ended questions about their experiences to learn and validate our persona.Additionally, this information helped narrow our focus for the goals and need of the company to gain more adoptions.

"The paperwork is what took the most time, so maybe if there was a way to make that process easier or quicker.."
"Inputing information into the system to make the adoption process faster"
"I would want to learn more about the dogs and see the dogs. Maybe some videos of them."

Collected Data from Interviews

With this information we were able to identify common problems across our interviews. With this, key insights were gained to assist our understanding of what our users needs are.

User Flow

After condusting user interviews, gaining insights, and narrowing down on what our goals are, our team established a user flow.

With this user flow, we wanted to know was journey Gerald would talk when on the browser or mobile app.

Sketches

Using my understanding of our goals, user, and user interaction, I developed sketches of the SA Humane Society redesigned mobile app.

Low Fidelity Wireframes

After many iterations, my team was able to begin the process of designing interfaces and layouts for the mobile app. Once our ideas were sketched out, we tested our prototypes to ensure the flow of the app is intuitive for our users.

High Fidelity WireFrames

We continued our interviews with user testing. We were able to conduct user testings in a remote environment over zoom. The users were asked to complete three tasks, and to follow, were also asked their thoughts of the app. During the user testing, users were able to let us know their experience while navigating through the app and web design.
